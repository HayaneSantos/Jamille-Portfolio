Case Study

FoodMarble

6-week Discovery Plan

OVERVIEW

FoodMarble created a breath analyser that, connected to its companion app, allows users to track their digestion. During my time at the company I worked in many different projects.
One of them was the 6-week Discovery Plan(6WDP).

THE CHALLENGE

Regular users found it hard to continue logging everything in the app and end up
not getting useful data and information about their digestion.
Some of them also needed more guidance on how to use the app, how to log meals and symptoms, take breath tests etc.
This problem was brought up by the CX team based on theirconversations with several users.

USER GOLS

  • Hand holding during the entire process, teaching them how to use the app.
  • More feedback along the way to keep them motivated.
  • Highlight the importance of daily logging to get valuable results.

BUSINESS GOALS

  • Engage users for longer when they first sign up.
  • Encourage them to finish the entire program in order to get a good
    understanding of their digestion.

MY ROLE

TOOLS USED

First Steps

Firstly, we needed to decide what this plan would look like. After a number of
discussions with the App and Clinical team, it was decided that a 6-week
period would be ideal to achieve valuable results and get enough insights for a
better understanding of user's digestion.
We then did a mind mapping with the different scenarios, user journeys and
different screens we'd have to create.

Design Process

WIREFRAMING

With the mind mapping done, I proceeded to the wireframes. When starting to
lay out all of the different screens, we had to go back to the mind map and
make a few changes we hadn't taken into account before.

Design Process

TUTORIALS

In order to help users log more data, we've added full tutorials guiding them
through the entire logging process and understanding every feature of the app.

Design Process

FEEDBACK

We've also implemented Daily and Weekly Summaries to keep users motivated
and to remind them that the data they put in is valuable.

USER INTERFACE AND PROTOTYPING

After working on the microcopy and user interface, a prototype was created to
enable testing the flow and the whole user experience prior to development.

High Fidelity Prototype

Outcome

When looking at the comparison between 6WDP users and non-user's
retention, we can see that, despite a gradual drop after the first week, user
retention was higher for users who had the plan than those who didn't have it.
Users were also more aware of the importance of logging data daily and
logged more breath tests, meals and symptoms.
